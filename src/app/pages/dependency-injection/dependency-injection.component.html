Dependency Injection (DI) pattern

Angular utilizes the DI pattern to manage the dependencies between components and services. With DI, the required dependencies are provided to a component or service through constructor injection or property injection, promoting loose coupling and testability.

Benefits of using the Dependency Injection pattern include:

  • Improved testability: Dependencies can easily be replaced with mock or stub implementations during testing, allowing for isolated unit tests.
  • Increased modularity: Classes become more modular and easier to understand since dependencies are explicitly declared.
  • Flexibility: Dependencies can be easily swapped or updated without modifying the dependent class.
  • Encourages best practices: Encourages adherence to the Single Responsibility Principle and other SOLID principles.

Overall, the Dependency Injection pattern helps in building scalable, maintainable, and testable software systems by reducing tight coupling between components.
