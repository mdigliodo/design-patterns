Design Patterns in Angular

Hello everyone!

Welcome to this article! Together, we're going to explore the interesting world of software development: design patterns. Throughout this journey, we'll look at different important patterns, like Singleton and Strategy, and learn about others such as observer, dependency injection, facade, composite, decorator, and factory. Let's dive in and enjoy learning together!

The main purpose of this article is to provide you with a clear and concise understanding of each of these patterns, exploring their descriptions, advantages, and practical applications in the context of web development with Angular.

I drew from a variety of sources to craft this content, but I want to especially acknowledge the valuable contribution of the article "Design Patterns in Angular" from DevGenius Blog, which I relied on as a guide and reference in my process of studying and understanding these concepts. DevGenius Blog

It's important for me to highlight that this article not only aims to be an educational resource for those looking to delve into the knowledge of design patterns but also a means to share my learning and experience with the developer community.

So, I invite you to delve into these pages with an open and curious mind, ready to discover how design patterns can improve the structure, modularity, and maintainability of your Angular projects. Enjoy the journey!
